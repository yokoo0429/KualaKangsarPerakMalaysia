HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE


Because of its close relationship to the Perak royal family, Kuala Kangsar is frequently referred to as the Royal Town of Perak. The Ubudiah Mosque, a magnificent example of Moorish and Victorian architecture, and the royal residence, Istana Iskandariah, are located there. In addition to their historical significance, these sites are magnificent representations of Islamic art and architecture.



1.Istana Iskandariah


Built between 1913 and 1917, the mosque was designed by Arthur Benison Hubback, an English architect who was also responsible for the design of Ipoh Railway Station, also known as Taj Mahal Ipoh. As a result of this collaboration, Masjid Ubudiah has been designated a National Heritage under the National Heritage Act 2005.

3.Istana Kenangan (Muzium Diraja Kuala Kangsar)


Explore the rich history of the Perak Sultanate by exploring this magnificent gallery, which combines neo-classical, Renaissance, and Moorish architectural styles. The gallery, which was once known as Istana Hulu and Istana Kota, is dedicated to the life and times of Sultan Azlan Shah, the 34th Sultan of Perak and the only monarch to hold the esteemed office of Lord President of the Federal Court, the highest court in Malaysia. Fascinating personal exhibitions of Sultan Azlan Shah, which include his opulent cars, clothes, accessories, bags, and even sunglasses, are among the highlights.


4. Galeri Sultan Azlan Shah

Coming back to 1897, this well-liked location for photography in Karai spans the Perak River and is the oldest railway bridge in Malaysia. It has served as a railway track for passenger trains carrying tin ore until its closure in 2002, as well as trips from Perai, Penang to Johor Bahru in 1910. In honour of Queen Victoria, Victoria Bridge was named during her Diamond Jubilee Year.
